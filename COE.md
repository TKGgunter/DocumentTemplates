# Summary
Introduce the problem service: what the service is used for, how it impacts
customers, who owns the service. Introduce the incident: when did it
occur, why did it occur, how did the responding team find out about the
incident, how did the incident impact customers, how was the incident resolved.
Each of the questions above should be answer in a sentence or two.

Use data in place of descriptive language. A large impact should become
impacted XXX number of droplets.

# Metrics and Graphs
Identify key graphs that paint a picture of the incident and the various issues
with the service during the incident. Label, and highlight sections of the
graph so that it is clear to the reader when the event occurred and the
resulting impact.  Be careful, graphs can be used as framing devices. An
unclear graph can frame the ensuing conversation in an unproductive way.

# Customer Impact
Quantify the amount and duration of the customer impact. Dependant services are
also customers.

Identify how impact could be reduced. Game a similar event and redesign your on
call practices or service design to deduce impact by a factor of two.
